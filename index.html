What is BOSS ?

BetaLabs will be conducting a mock GSoC, Betalabs OpenSource Season (BOSS) for all the students to familiarize themselves with the procedures of participating in such programs and gain experience for the actual GSOC. This mainly serves as a learning experience. The mock GSOC will take place in mid-February. In order to participate, you can take a look at the following projects, choose one to participate in, and start learning that specific tech stack to contribute.

F.A.Qs

Q:

How do I contribute to the projects ?

A:

The projects will be hosted on Github, where the issues in the projects would be listed with the appropiate tags. Contributors can take up these issues, according to their knowledge and interests. They can always feel free to contact the maintainers with genuine doubts about the projects.

Q:

Do I have to submit Proposals for Beginner Issues?

A:

Proposals are not required for Beginner issues, you can directly take it up, after informing the maintainers for that project. However, this applies only to the issues, which are tagged as 'Beginner Friendly', on the project repo.

Q:

What would I get from participating in BOSS?

A:

You will get valuable experience working on open souce projects and it would be a good practive for the real Gsoc. For the Freshers, you can use this time to explore new technologies and interact with their seniors, while contibuting to the projects you can.
